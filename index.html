About Me

Hello! I'm Leigh Cohen, a dynamic and dedicated individual whose fascination with mathematics led to a fulfilling career with a unique blend of international teaching and data analysis experience. My journey into the realms of machine learning and data science ignited during my university days at UC Davis, where I immersed myself in Mathematical and Scientific Computation. After graduation, I embarked on a transformative two-year teaching experience in Thailand, where I taught Math and English to students from 1st grade to 9th grade, along with imparting knowledge to Thai teachers in the vibrant towns of Samut Prakhan and Chiang Mai. All throughout this time, I coupled my teaching commitment with continuous learning and practice in data analysis and engineering through various online projects. This experience expanded my appreciation for the global impact of mathematics and data analysis in education.

Returning to the US, my high school math teaching ventures in San Francisco unveiled the powerful intersection of storytelling with numeric and logic-based realms. This revelation fueled my interest in data science—the art of revealing hidden insights within data, crafting compelling narratives, and driving impactful outcomes for institutions and businesses.

Data science forms the basis for my thought models, fostering continuous learning from the world around me. Now, I'm eager to leverage my extensive knowledge and experience as a Machine Learning/Data Engineer. My goal is to contribute to the evolution of institutions and businesses through the captivating art of storytelling.

My teaching experience not only strengthened my mathematical foundation but also honed my communication, teamwork, problem-solving, and creative thinking skills. I've been actively involved in various projects, primarily utilizing SQL and Python. You can explore my latest work on my portfolio below and more projects on my GitHub profile.

Beyond the world of data science, you'll find me immersed in diverse interests—playing the piano, practicing Jiu-Jitsu, teaching or practicing yoga, and embarking on exciting outdoor adventures.

Creating-Maps-and-Visualizing-Geospatial-Data-of-SF-Crime-and-Immigration-to-Canada in 1980-2013

In these projects, I generate interactive maps to display data on crime in SF as well as immigration from various countries into Canada duringi the years 1980-2013.
In the first project, I use a world map with various types of markers and clusters of the markers to represent different types of crime in specific locations of SF according to their longitude and latitude.
In the second project, I depict international migration flows from countries all around the world to Canada during the years of 1980-2013 using a Choropleth map to convey the varying intensity of immigration according to the darkness of the colors of each country.
I discover that China and India have the highest numbers of immigrants to Canada during this time period, with the Phillipines following close behind.

Computational Evidence for a Two-stage Rivalry Hierarchy in Vision

In this project, the question at hand is "how is vision rivalry structured and when will the rivalry make a jump from traditional monocular vision to higher binocular vision?". I used a system of differential equations to computationally model rivalry in both monocular and binocular-driven vision. The model in this study shows that there are two stages in the rivalry hierarchy, one led by traditional monocular rivalry and the second by alternating binocular rivalry.
