The Importance of Human Activity Recognition Using Smartphones

A series of experiments were conducted to evaluate the ability of the HAR smartphone application to identify different basic human motions, with the goal of providing useful information on potential health risks and problems such as motion disorders. Using methods such as PCA and Kmeans clustering, I was able to conclude that the HAR application is able to distinguish between some activities, yet isn't strong enough to accurately distinguish between all the activities measured.

Computational Evidence for a Two-stage Rivalry Hierarchy in Vision

In this project, the question at hand is "how is vision rivalry structured and when will the rivalry make a jump from traditional monocular vision to higher binocular vision?". I used a system of differential equations to computationally model rivalry in both monocular and binocular-driven vision. The model in this study shows that there are two stages in the rivalry hierarchy, one led by traditional monocular rivalry and the second by alternating binocular rivalry.
